notation, conventions, etc.

A note on rules

The DM has the final say on a whether or not, or how, a rule applies. In the event of confusion, the DM's decision will apply for the rest of the session.
Discussion of meta-mechanics must happen out-of-character, and must not interrupt the session.

A note on powergaming

I'm fine with powergaming and min-maxing as long as you adhere to the spirit of D&D, that is, as long as you are:

  • having fun,
  • playing your role,
  • helping others have fun, or
  • helping others play their role.

I'm completely fine with trying to be as powerful as possible, but D&D is not a game you can win, so don't treat it like you can lose either.
Cheating is never OK.

Dice

I use the standard die notation, that is [number]d[faces]+[modifier], for example 4d6+2 means to roll 4 six-sided dice, and add 2 to the sum.
I also use notation for discarding some dice, d[number]h and d[number]l to drop a number of the highest or lowest dice respectively.
For example, the standard roll for generating stats (4d6, only take the three highest rolls) would be represented as 4d6dl.

Where only one die is rolled or dropped, 1 may be omitted. For example d6 instead of 1d6, or 4d6dl instead of 4d6d1l.
